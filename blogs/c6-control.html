Control - Riding your emotions like a horse

As the title suggests, our emotions are very much like riding a horse, they have to be tamed and understood in order to avoid galloping off and spiralling out of control. I enjoyed reading about self regulation and the art of 'Letting Go' which reminded me of the saying 'water off a duck's back'. Of course, not everything experienced can be brushed aside so easily, hence the practice of re-enacting the triggers which have caused distress. By practising the Siberian North Railroad technique your body and mind builds up a mechasism to deal and alleviate negative or distressing emotions. The key takeaway for me is "Don't feed the monsters"!

My two minutes of mindfulness calms the mind from the ongoing battle that its been pitched against. By concentrating on my breathing and body scanning, this unease subsides - although getting to bed earlier would certainly help.
